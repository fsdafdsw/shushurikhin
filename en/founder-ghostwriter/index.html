Ghostwriter for founders and subject-matter experts

I write founder pieces, bylined essays, letters, and thought-leadership articles that need to sound like a real person, not a cleaned-up committee draft.

Best fit

Founders, executives, operators, and specialists who have something real to say but do not want to spend weeks wrestling it into a publishable draft.

The core of the work is voice, structure, and credibility under close reading.

01 / Fit

When ghostwriting makes more sense than pure editing

Usually when the substance exists, the point of view exists, and the person exists, but the draft either does not exist yet or still sounds unlike its supposed author.

For founders and CEOs

Pieces that need clear judgment, stable tone, and enough authority to stand outside the company bubble.

For experts and operators

Subject-matter pieces that should feel articulate and deliberate rather than translated from a rough call transcript.

For thought leadership

Work that needs a point of view, not just competent prose.

02 / Process

How the work usually runs

Good ghostwriting starts with the person rather than the document: how they reason, what they emphasize, what they would never say, and what they are actually trying to move.

What I take on

  • Turning calls, notes, voice memos, and fragments into a coherent piece.
  • Finding the right form: bylined essay, founder note, letter, or thought-leadership article.
  • Shaping the voice so the piece feels authored rather than merely polished.

What needs to be clear early

  • Who the piece is for.
  • What the piece needs to change, signal, or defend.
  • Which parts of the voice are essential and which are negotiable.

04 / FAQ

What people usually worry about

The central concern is usually not whether someone else can write the piece. It is whether the result will still sound like the person behind it.

How do you preserve the actual voice?

Through calls, source material, constraints, and a clear sense of what belongs to the person versus what belongs to the page.

Can this work without endless calls?

Yes. One solid conversation plus good material is often enough to start.

Does this only apply to founders?

No. The same logic works for operators, researchers, consultants, and specialists whose work needs a more public voice.

Can this work for English-facing audiences?

Yes. That is exactly the point of this version of the site.
