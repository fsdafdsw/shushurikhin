02 / Setup

What the workflow usually includes

Not one magic prompt. A working system: brief, examples, negative examples, review points, tone rules, and a human release layer.

What can be built

  • Prompt systems for specific content types.
  • Brand and tone guardrails.
  • Editorial QA checkpoints before publication.
  • Review logic that leaves humans with meaningful decisions rather than cleanup drudgery.

What that changes

  • More consistent output across different people.
  • Less rework on obvious tonal failures.
  • Cleaner drafts for human editors.
  • Better odds of publishing without embarrassment.

04 / FAQ

What people usually ask first

Mostly whether this is about prompting or editing. In practice, it is about both.

Is this mostly about prompts?

No. Prompts matter, but prompts without review logic still produce fragile output.

Can this be adapted to a specific brand voice?

Yes. That is the entire point of building examples, constraints, and review checkpoints into the workflow.

Do humans still need to review the work?

Yes. The goal is better human review, not a fantasy of no human review.

Is this only for large teams?

No. Small teams and solo operators often benefit from guardrails even more.
