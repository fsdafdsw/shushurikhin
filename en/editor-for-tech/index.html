Editor for tech, AI, and complex B2B topics

I write and edit technical explainers, category pieces, and product-adjacent articles that need to stay clear without losing precision.

This is usually the right fit when a company has real expertise, but the draft still sounds too internal, too jargon-heavy, or too close to the source material.

Typical use cases

Technical explainers, AI topics, product narratives, infrastructure writing, B2B blog posts, and expert-led bylined pieces.

Especially useful when a company needs sharper thinking on the page rather than more volume.

01 / Fit

Where this kind of editorial work matters most

Usually where a subject is technically dense, commercially important, and headed toward readers who will stop reading the second the writing feels vague.

Product and platform writing

When a technical subject has to be explained to a buyer, operator, partner, or broader market without becoming simplistic.

B2B category content

When a company needs category clarity, not just another article that paraphrases the website.

Expert-led pieces

When the piece is attached to a CTO, founder, architect, or specialist and has to sound like it came from a credible adult.

02 / Output

What that usually turns into

Most often: explainers, expert articles, category pieces, and longform B2B writing with a stronger editorial backbone.

Common formats

  • Technical explainers for broader audiences.
  • B2B blog posts and thought-leadership pieces.
  • Bylined articles from internal experts.
  • Longform pieces around product, infrastructure, or AI.

What I am usually fixing

  • Internal language that should not survive the first public draft.
  • Structure that makes sense to the team but not to the reader.
  • Tone that sounds either too dry or too generic.
  • Arguments that need cleaner sequencing and stronger emphasis.

04 / FAQ

Questions that usually come up first

The usual questions are about depth, accuracy, and whether the piece can still feel human once the subject gets technical.

Do I need to bring a draft?

No. Notes, internal docs, a working call, or access to the right expert can be enough.

Can you work with highly technical material?

Yes, provided there is access to the substance. My job is not to invent expertise, but to shape it into a readable piece.

Is this only for company blogs?

No. It can also be for bylined articles, media submissions, category pieces, and product-adjacent thought leadership.

Can this become an ongoing editorial line?

Yes. In many cases the real value is consistency across a run of pieces, not a single isolated article.
