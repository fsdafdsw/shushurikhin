04 / Practice

How I usually work

The English-language version is positioned a bit closer to the way clients in the US, UK, and other English-speaking markets usually buy this work: not “content,” but founder voice, expert writing, thought leadership, and high-trust editorial output.

Common engagements

  • Founder and executive ghostwriting.
  • Tech and B2B articles that explain a complex subject cleanly.
  • Longform editing for essays, features, and flagship pieces.
  • Editorial review for AI-assisted content workflows.

How the work tends to run

  • First we pin down the audience, the context, and what the piece needs to do.
  • Then I shape the structure, the argument, and the tone that makes the piece hold together.
  • The final output should be something you can publish, defend internally, or send to a publication without embarrassment.

06 / FAQ

Questions that usually come up first

Most early questions are not about format, but about voice, depth, and whether the work can hold up in front of a demanding reader.

Do I need a draft before reaching out?

No. Notes, voice memos, rough structure, or a good call can be enough to start.

Do you work in English natively enough for public-facing writing?

Yes. The goal is not “translated English,” but clean, natural copy that reads comfortably in an English-language context.

Is this mostly for startups and tech?

That is the strongest commercial fit in English, but the underlying work is broader: expert writing, editorial shaping, and high-trust prose.

Can this be ongoing rather than one-off?

Yes. Ongoing work often makes more sense when a company needs a stronger editorial line rather than a single isolated asset.
